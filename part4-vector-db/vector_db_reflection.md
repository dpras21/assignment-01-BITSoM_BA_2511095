
## Vector DB Use Case

A traditional keyword-based search system may not be sufficient for a law firm that needs to search through large 500-page contracts using natural language questions. Keyword search relies on exact word matching and may fail to understand the semantic meaning of legal language. For example, a lawyer asking “What are the termination clauses?” may miss relevant sections that use different terminology such as “agreement cancellation conditions.”

Vector databases solve this problem by converting text into embeddings that capture semantic relationships between words and sentences. This allows the system to retrieve conceptually similar content even if the exact keywords are not present. In legal documents, where the same idea can be expressed in multiple ways, semantic search improves accuracy and efficiency.

In this system, contracts can be broken into smaller chunks, converted into vector embeddings, and stored in a vector database. When a lawyer asks a question, the query is also converted into an embedding and compared using similarity search to find the most relevant sections instantly.

Therefore, a vector database plays a crucial role in enabling intelligent document search, improving productivity, and reducing the time required to manually scan long legal contracts.
